How it works

Real data first, model-like reasoning second.

RxNorm

Name normalization

The app searches NIH RxNorm so common medicine names map to standardized identifiers.

RxNav

Interaction scan

When identifiers are found, the app checks public interaction records and presents source descriptions.

Rules

Safety context

Local rules flag duplicate entries, missing age/pregnancy/organ-function context, and high-risk medicine keywords.

Safety limits

This is decision support, not medical advice.

Do not stop, start, or change medicine because of this app alone. If there are severe symptoms such as trouble breathing, chest pain, fainting, swelling of the face/throat, or signs of overdose, seek emergency care immediately.

The app can miss interactions when the medicine name is unknown, the drug is local-only, the issue is dose-specific, or the patient context is incomplete. It is built to improve questions, not to replace professionals.
